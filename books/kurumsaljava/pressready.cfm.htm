Press-Ready PDFs- Black and White Books

If you are providing PDFs of your book, interior and cover, you'll need to follow these guidelines to make sure that you provide acceptable files. If you are unable to follow these instructions you should use one of our templates or custom design services.

The file that represents the interior of your book is called a bookblock. The book jacket is simply referred to as the cover. Your cover and the bookblock will be checked against our specifications. If your files are not correct you will be notified to resubmit your files.

* * * * * *

Book Interior File- Bookblock

At this point, your manuscript should be 100% spell-checked and proofed, including correcting homonyms (carrot vs caret). There are no revisions made without incurring a resubmission fee.

It is recommended that you use a page layout application such as Quark Xpress or Adobe InDesign to lay out your book. These applications offer greater flexibility and control in terms of creating layouts and producing postscript files (.ps) for output and then conversion to .PDF using Adobe Acrobat Distiller. Word processing applications can also be used but they are less flexible when used to create book layouts.

In either case, you are creating a layout in one application that will then be output as a PDF.

For printing purposes, regardless of what size book you're publishing, the final PDF of your bookblock has to have a page dimension of 8.5"x11" (letter). Your layout has to be centered in this letter-sized page, horizontally and vertically (equal spaces on top, bottom and right and left).

If you're using a page layout application, you can create your layout at the actual trim size (5"x8", 5.5"x8.5" or 6"x9") and then as you output your PDF you can make the paper size 8.5"x11" in the Print dialogue window in your page layout application.

If you're creating your bookblock in a word processing program, you have to start with an 8.5"x11" document and use the Document window to adjust the margins according to your actual trim size.

Odd pages (1, 3, 5,...) are right-hand pages, and even pages (2, 4, 6,...) are left-hand pages. The gutter is the inside margin of your book, the edge that is bound and becomes the spine. It is customary to add more space to this margin to compensate for the pinch of the binding. .6" to .8" should be enough depending on your pagecount and personal preference.

For any size book, your margins should be approximately .5" on the top, bottom and outside (the margin opposite the gutter). You can adjust these as you see fit. Just remember to allow a comfortable margin for the body of your text. Also, compensate for your header or footer as you may want to include folios (page numbers) and your name and title.

Keep all text approximately .25" in from the trim. If you have an element that bleeds off the edge of the page, make sure that it extends at least .25" outside the trim.

Word Processing Applications
If you're using a word processing application to create your layout you'll need to make sure that your margins not only reflect what you want for your layout but also take into consideration the extra area of the 8.5"x11" page size. For instance, if you are creating a 6"w x9"h book, your text will probably cover a 5"x8" area with a .5" top and bottom margin. That's 3" total of extra space, top and bottom outside of your text area. Divide that in half and you have your top and bottom margins of 1.5" each. The same thing applies to your outside margin and gutter. Add the extra space on the left and right to your layout's margins, approximately 2" each side.

If you do not have a way to generate a .PDF file from your word processing application you may be able to use this free utility.

Graphics
Any graphics (photos, images, illustrations, logos...) that you include in your bookblock have to be 300dpi and grayscale. Saving all images as grayscale .jpgs before importing them to your layout is recommended.

Fonts
All fonts used by your layout have to be embedded in your PDF file.

Exporting to a .PDF File
We highly recommend that you use Adobe Acrobat Distiller to produce your final PDF as it gives you more options and control. If using Adobe Acrobat Distiller, use the default Press Quality setting. Under Edit Adobe PDF Settings, Images, turn off Sampling for Grayscale Images. Under Edit Adobe PDF Settings, Color, select Leave Color Unchanged in the Color Management Policies field. In the Device Dependent Data field, select Preserve for "When transfer functions are found" and check the box that says Preserve Halftone Information. This should help guarantee producing a high-resolution grayscale PDF as needed.

If you are exporting your bookblock directly to PDF without use of Adobe Acrobat Distiller, eliminate all other colors in your layout document and make sure that all of your text is 100% black.

To make sure that your text prints as you expect it to, make sure that you use the actual fonts for italics and bolds as opposed to the application italic or bold. Word processors have buttons for italic and bold that actually are fake italics and bolds. These are simulations of those fonts.

Cover Preparation

All covers must be submitted as a single PDF file with the image centered. Covers must be submitted at 300 dpi, CMYK, one-sided.

All covers should be designed in CMYK. RGB has a wider range of color, but printing is done in CMYK. If you start designing in RGB you may fall in love with a color that cannot be reproduced in CMYK and be disappointed.

Whatever size book you're publishing make sure that all text is within .25" of the trim, or edge. If you have artwork that bleeds, allow an extra .25" of image outside the trim. Design your cover from right to left. Whatever is on the front cover will be on the right with the spine and back cover to the left, in that order. Depending on the your final pagecount and the width of your book's spine, the back cover should shift further to the left as needed.

Hardcovers
Hardcover book jackets include 3.25" flaps extending from the back and front covers. Place your text on a safe area that is 2.5"x8" on each flap, making sure to butt the text block .25" from the back cover (to the right) and front cover (to the left).

Also, add .25" between your covers (front and back) and your flaps to compensate for the wrap when your book jacket folds over the hardcover.

Spine
We recommend that you use a solid color background for your spine.

Calculating Spines for Perfectbound Books
Calculate your spine width using this equation: Page count divided by 488 (PPI of the paper stock) plus .03 (1/32 of an inch) to allow for the cover stock and glue between pages. Example: 200 pages/488= 0.41+0.03= 0.44 inches

Spines for Hardcover Books:
Hardcover spines are wider due to the thickness of the covers and therefore require a different calculation. Take your page count and divide it by our paper's ppi (pages per inch) which is 488. Then add .25 to your subtotal to compensate for the thickness of your hardcover. Example: 200 pages/488= 0.41+0.25= 0.66 inches

So, counting from the left to the right on your cover, you should have a total of a 3.25" (with a 2.5"x8" text safe area) flap, a .25" wraparound buffer, your back cover which should be 5.5" wide, your spine (page count ÷ 488 + .25" = ???"), your front cover (5.5"), another wraparound buffer of .25" and the front cover flap of 3.25". You should have a total of 18.5" plus your actual spine width.

Barcode
Your barcode has to fall in a designated space on the bottom of your back cover. Please allow a 1.25"h x 2"w blank area for the barcode (unless using the managed printing service with your own ISBN). This blank area should be .375" from the bottom trim line of your back cover and centered between the left trim line and the spine. This blank area is approximately 6.5" from the top trim line for a 5"x8" book, 7" for a 5.5"x8.5" book, and 7.75" for a 6"x9" book.
